How to Remove Adult Content from IPTV Playlists

December 8, 2025 5 min read

TL;DR: Use Strim to search for and disable groups containing "XXX", "Adult", "18+", and similar terms. Export the filtered playlist for family-friendly viewing.

The Problem with IPTV Playlists

Many IPTV providers include adult content in their playlists, often mixed in with regular programming. This can be problematic if you:

  • Have children who use the same TV or device
  • Share your IPTV subscription with family members
  • Simply don't want that content in your playlist
  • Use IPTV in shared spaces like living rooms

The good news: You can easily filter this content out using Strim, creating a clean, family-friendly playlist.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Load Your Playlist

  1. Open Strim
  2. Paste your M3U playlist URL or raw text
  3. Wait for the playlist to load and parse

Step 2: Identify Adult Content Groups

Adult content is typically organized into groups with recognizable names. Use the search box to find groups containing:

Common Adult Group Names to Search

XXX Adult 18+ Mature For Adults NSFW Playboy Hustler

Note: Group names vary by provider. Some may use abbreviations, foreign language terms, or euphemisms. Review all groups carefully.

Step 3: Disable Adult Groups

  1. Search for "XXX" in the groups filter
  2. Toggle off any matching groups
  3. Repeat with "Adult", "18+", and other terms
  4. Review remaining groups for any you missed

Step 4: Export Your Clean Playlist

Once you've disabled all adult content groups:

  • Download the filtered playlist as a .m3u file
  • Or generate a shareable URL for your IPTV app
  • Use this clean playlist on family devices

Alternative Approach: Whitelist Only

For maximum safety, instead of removing adult content, you can create a whitelist of only approved channels:

  1. Click "Deselect All" to disable everything
  2. Manually enable only groups you approve:
    • News channels
    • Sports
    • Kids/Cartoons
    • Family-friendly movies
    • Educational content
  3. Export this curated, safe playlist

Pro Tip: This whitelist approach is more thorough because it only includes content you've explicitly approved, rather than trying to filter out everything inappropriate.

Additional Safety Tips

Use App-Level Parental Controls

Many IPTV apps (TiviMate, IPTV Smarters) have built-in parental controls. Enable these as an additional layer of protection.

Device-Level Controls

Use your device's parental controls (Fire TV, Apple TV, Android TV) to restrict app access or require PIN codes.

Separate Devices

Consider having a separate streaming device for kids that only has the filtered kids playlist installed.

Re-Filter Regularly

IPTV providers update their channels frequently. Re-filter your playlist monthly to catch any new adult content groups.

Conclusion

Creating a family-friendly IPTV playlist is straightforward with Strim. Whether you remove specific adult groups or create a whitelist of approved channels, you can ensure everyone in your household has a safe viewing experience.
